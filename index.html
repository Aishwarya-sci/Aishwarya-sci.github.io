About me

Scientist.
Curious human.

I'm a PhD researcher based in Israel, working at the intersection of marine biology, microbiology, and computational modeling. My research focuses on large benthic foraminifera — single-celled organisms with beautiful shells — as indicators of coral reef health.

My days involve fieldwork in the Red Sea, hours under the microscope, image analysis, and writing more than I ever expected a scientist to write. I find this work endlessly fascinating.

Outside the lab, I cook (South Asian food, always), read, make art, travel when I can, and firmly believe that a good cup of chai is its own kind of methodology.

Research

What I study &
why it matters

Coral reefs are among the most biodiverse and threatened ecosystems on Earth. I use foraminifera — ancient, shell-forming protists — as living records of reef health across time and depth.

🐚

Seasonal Ecology of Foram Communities

Tracking how foram assemblages shift across seasons and depths, and what those shifts reveal about reef conditions in the Gulf of Aqaba.

🔬

Symbiosis & Microbiome Flexibility

Investigating how foraminifera associate with algal and bacterial partners — and whether those relationships are more flexible than we thought.

🌿

Epiphytic Foraminifera & Kleptoplasty

Exploring Planogypsina — a poorly understood species that may retain functional chloroplasts from algae it consumes, a phenomenon called kleptoplasty.
